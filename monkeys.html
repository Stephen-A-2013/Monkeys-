Different Types of Monkeys

Capuchin Monkey

Capuchin monkeys are small New World monkeys native to Central and South America. They are known for their intelligence and often used in movies and television.

Mandrill

Mandrills are large monkeys found in equatorial Africa. They are famous for their bright colored faces and rumps, and are the largest species of monkey.

Spider Monkey

Spider monkeys are found in tropical forests of Central and South America. They are known for their long limbs and prehensile tail, which they use to swing between trees.

Howler Monkey

Howler monkeys are recognized by their loud, distinctive calls that can be heard for miles. They are native to South and Central American forests.

Japanese Macaque

Japanese macaques, also known as snow monkeys, are native to Japan. They are well known for living in cold climates and bathing in hot springs.
